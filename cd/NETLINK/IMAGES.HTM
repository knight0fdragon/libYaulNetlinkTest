Images And Sound


The Net Link web browser can display computer pictures that were saved in many different formats, including GIF89a (including animations), JPEG (typically used for photos), and XBM (a monochrome UNIX image style) image file formats. You don't need to know what kind of picture is coming from the Web - your Net Link will automatically determine the format and display it properly. You can view these kinds of images inside of Web pages, and you can view these images by themselves. Images up to approximately 400 kBytes in size can be displayed.



You can also listen to audio (sound) files that were recorded in several different standard formats, including WAV (a Windows format), AIFF (a Macintosh format), and AU (a UNIX format). As with pictures, you don't need to know what kind of format the audio file was recorded in - your Net Link will automatically determine the format and play it properly. When you select one of these audio files, the progress bar on the lower left will show you the completion percentage -- sometimes audio files can be quite large and take a long time to download. You can listen to audio files of up to approximately 400 kBytes in size.



Once the audio file has been loaded, you can Play, Fast Forward, Fast Rewind, Stop, and Pause the sound file from the Audio Control Panel. You can also jump into the middle of the file by clicking on the Waveform.
